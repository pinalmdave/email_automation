# Reply Templates by Recruiter Intent

Guidance for composing `reply_body` based on the classified intent.
Templates are starting points — tailor tone to the recruiter's style, but
keep the factual content (compensation posture, visa status, location)
consistent with `pinal_profile.md`.

## SALARY_INQUIRY — asked about compensation / rate
Do **not** give a specific number.

> Thank you for reaching out. I am open to discussing compensation that
> aligns with the role's scope, responsibilities, and market standards.
> Once I understand the full details of the position and benefits, I am
> happy to go into specifics on a call.

If the recruiter insists on a range:

> Based on the scope of a senior/architect-level role, I would expect
> compensation in the range typical for this market in New Jersey / NYC.
> Happy to firm up specifics on a call.

## AVAILABILITY — interview slot or start date
Interview:

> I am available for an interview at your earliest convenience and can
> accommodate most time slots during business hours (ET). Please share a
> few options and I will confirm.

Start date:

> I can start within 2–3 weeks of accepting an offer, pending the standard
> notice period with my current employer.

## TECHNICAL_QUESTIONS — skills / experience depth
- Answer from Pinal's real experience (see `pinal_profile.md`).
- Reference relevant projects at **Hexaware** or **Fox News** when they fit.
- Be specific and confident — numbers and stack details beat vague claims.
- **Never fabricate** experience.

## SCHEDULE_INTERVIEW — recruiter proposes a time
If they gave specific times, confirm one and thank them. If open-ended:

> Thank you for setting up the interview. I am available during standard
> business hours (ET). Please share a few options and I will confirm.

## VISA_STATUS — work authorization
Always clear and unambiguous:

> I am a US Green Card (GC) holder and am authorized to work in the US
> without sponsorship.

## LOCATION_RELOCATION — where can you work
> I am based in New Jersey and open to remote, hybrid, or onsite roles.
> I am also willing to relocate for the right opportunity.

## JOB_DETAILS — recruiter sharing more info
Express continued interest. Ask clarifying questions on anything vague:
team size, tech stack, remote policy, contract vs. FTE, compensation
structure (base vs. total), benefits.

## GENERAL_FOLLOWUP — "just checking in"
> Thank you for following up. I remain very interested in this opportunity
> and would love to move forward. Please let me know the next steps.

## REJECTION — position filled or moving on
Keep it gracious — recruiters remember polite candidates:

> Thank you for the update and for your time. I appreciate the opportunity
> and would love to stay in touch for future roles that align with my
> experience.

## OTHER — anything else
Use professional judgment. Default tone: direct, warm, concise,
respectful of the recruiter's time. Never commit to anything that
contradicts `pinal_profile.md`.

## Universal rules
- Sign off with **"Thanks, Pinal Dave"** (or **"Best regards, Pinal Dave"**
  for more formal recruiters).
- Include LinkedIn only when introducing himself or when asked.
- Keep replies under ~150 words unless the recruiter's question demands more.
- Match the recruiter's formality level — mirror their greeting style.
